What is FOSS and Why Should You Care?

As the name says, the software is Free and Open Source. Not free as in price but in terms of the freedom which lets you modify and redistribute it based on the license, without having to worry about the legal issues. Open Source is a concept where the source code of the software is open for all which is the opposite to closed source. In case of the closed source the source code is not open to the public except for the developer of the software or the company which distributes it. A software can be opensource and not free at the same time. I don't want to go too much into it. There are TONS of articles about this. I encourage you to read those and get the gist of it. I am happy to direct you to the articles which will give you a better insight into the meaning of the FOSS.

Why Should You Use FOSS?

  • Your Windows PC/Laptop has more chances of getting a trojan/virus than a PC/Laptop which is running Linux. I am not saying that Linux is immune to all the threat out there in the wild but it is better than what a closed source platform has to offer. No hate on Windows though but it falls short on fulfilling many aspects of the goals of an OS.

3.No more forced updates or crashes

  • Forced updates on Windows which can take from few minutes to several hours. During the updates your machine becomes unusable.
  • Users have no idea what gets updated or what gets Installed in case of the Windows.
  • Many proprietary softwares on the Windows constantly bother the user to update to the PRO version. Which is very annoying and unless you have the pro version the features of the application are limited.
  • In case of GNU/Linux there is not a single piece of software which gets installed against the will of the user. You are free to update and upgrade whenever you want. You can use your PC/Laptop during the updates. You can choose what to install and not to.
  • Linux can be configured and made to look as the way you like to be. Literally there is no limit to the customization you can do.

To stay more safe and private online:

  • Use DuckDuckgo instead of Google.
  • If you feel like you need more accurate results, use the startpage search engine.
  • Use an open source password manager like Bitwardern across devices, which helps you remember strong and unique passwords for all of your online accounts for free.
  • Use the Brave web browser to be protected against malicious ads and sites by default. Brave is a wonderful web-browser which will make your life easy. Its based on Chromium and it is also available on mobile devices.
  • Brave does come with few drawbacks and if you are more of a tech-savy person, you can use Firefox with add-ons like Privacy badger and No-Script.
  • As I recently noticed, the ISPs are trying to monetize on their users' browsing data by spying on them. To overcome this you can use a private DNS like 1.1.1.1 or any OpenNIC Domain Name Servers.
